Watch these (or read these if I leave a note about it). They are only in order of when it popped into my head.

Kaichou wa Maid-sama
Good, but the female main character is pretty much being bullied by the male main character the whole time. Don't mind the art style that promotional images give it. The actual art style in the content is a lot better. Only a bit under the first half, about up to chapter 32 in the manga, is covered by the anime. You'll need to read manga if you want the rest of the story. If you want to watch this with free streaming on youtube from North America, you'll need a VPN. I personally suggest relocating yourself to the UK.
My Teen Romantic Comedy SNAFU
This show is amazing. Watch it. The manga is pretty much exactly the same as the anime, and I don't believe English translations of the manga have even caught up to the anime, so no need to read the manga as well
Sword Art Online
Slice of life fantasy, done by using video games. The popular opinion states that the first half of SAO is far far better than the second half. SAO 2 isn't as good as SAO, but the first half of SAO 2 is pretty good. The main character seems like he's hitting on girls whenever he talks to a named female character. That is, with the exclusion of the person who he's actually in love it. Weird how anime works, right?.
Noucome (Direct Link)
You may want to use Noucome's full name, My Mental Choices are Completely Interfering with my School Romantic Comedy. This contains a lot of fanservice jokes, but it's still a pretty good comedy.
Tsubasa: Reservoir Chronicles
Anime does exist of it, but from what little I've seen of the anime, the manga is better. You're not weird if you don't get the story the first time reading it
White Album 2
Don't watch this. You will cry. That isn't a good thing. Really, don't. I put this here as a warning, not a suggestion. It's not a bad anime, you'll just be very sad when it's done, and if you attempt to look at visual novel summaries of the story after the end of the anime, you'll get even sadder.
Nisekoi
This is a funny little rom-com. I would definitely suggest it. It's a tsundere if you like that kind of thing.
Toradora!
Actually similar to maid-sama and nisekoi in some ways. Definitely a tsundere romance (as both of the aforementioned anime were).
Okami-san and Her Seven Companions
Funny narration. Incredibly absurd content.
